Q: Explain V-I characteristics of PN junction diode

Answer:

The V-I characteristics of a PN junction diode show the relationship between voltage and current:

  • Forward Bias: When P-side is positive, current increases exponentially after cut-in voltage (0.7V for Si, 0.3V for Ge)
  • Reverse Bias: Very small reverse saturation current flows until breakdown voltage
  • Breakdown Region: At high reverse voltage, current increases sharply (Zener breakdown)

Q: Compare BJT and JFET

Answer:

Key differences between BJT and JFET:

  • Control: BJT is current-controlled, JFET is voltage-controlled
  • Input Impedance: BJT has low, JFET has very high
  • Type: BJT is bipolar, JFET is unipolar
  • Noise: JFET has lower noise than BJT

Q: State and prove Thevenin's theorem

Answer:

Statement: Any linear bilateral network can be replaced by an equivalent circuit consisting of a voltage source Vth in series with resistance Rth.

Proof steps:

  • Remove load resistance
  • Calculate Vth (open circuit voltage)
  • Calculate Rth (short all sources)
  • Replace network with Vth and Rth
